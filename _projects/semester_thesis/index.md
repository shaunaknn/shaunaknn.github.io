title: Assessment and optimization of species transport for laminar Methane-Hydrogen flames in OpenFOAM

description:  Simulated laminar premixed 1D methane–hydrogen flames in CANTERA across varying hydrogen enrichments and equivalence ratios. Compared flame properties using the GRI 3.0 and García 3-Step mechanisms and analyzed hydrogen mass-fraction behavior across lean methane flames. Enhanced the in-house reactingFoam_DTF OpenFOAM solver by implementing diffusion-correction and correction-velocity terms. Implemented custom post-processing functions in C++ within the enhanced solver for improved flame-structure evaluation. Derived hydrogen mass-fraction profiles in 1D OpenFOAM flames using GRI 3.0 with unity Lewis number and reproduced results with the García 3-Step mechanism for 31% (v/v) lean hydrogen-enriched cases.Extended all 1D solver modifications to 2D Kornilov slit flames and assessed their impact across different flame locations. Quantified flame lift-off height, flame length, heat-release-rate errors, and hydrogen mass-fraction deviations for cases with and without correction terms.
